## Overview  
This project uses a large-scale Apple retail dataset representing transactional activity across multiple countries, stores, and product categories. It provides a realistic view of retail operations, enabling analysis of customer behavior, product performance, and revenue distribution across global markets.

The dataset includes:

- Transaction records (orders, quantities, revenue)  
- Customer information (IDs, demographics, purchasing patterns)  
- Product details (categories, attributes)  
- Store and regional data (country, location)  
- Warranty claims and service-related records  

These variables support both descriptive analytics and predictive modelling across customer, product, and operational dimensions.

---

### Models Used

- K-Means Clustering – for customer segmentation based on purchasing behaviour  
- RFM Analysis (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) – for customer value assessment  
- Conjoint Analysis – for understanding product attribute preferences  
- Logistic Regression – for predicting warranty claim risk  
- Time Series / Trend Analysis – for sales forecasting  

---

### Data Source  

The dataset was provided as part of an academic/business case study and simulates real-world Apple retail operations across international markets.

Note: The dataset is anonymised and structured for analytical and educational purposes.

---

### Key Analytical Opportunities  

The dataset enables exploration of:

- Customer segmentation and identification of high-value users  
- Product performance across regions and store locations  
- Drivers of customer purchase decisions  
- Prediction of warranty claim risk for operational planning  
- Sales trends and forecasting for inventory and revenue optimisation  

These analyses support data-driven decision making and provide insights into optimising retail portfolio strategy across global markets.
